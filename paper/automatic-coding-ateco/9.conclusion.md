# Conclusion

## Section Goal

Close the paper with a compact synthesis of what the study shows, why it matters, and what remains open.

## Main Points To Preserve

- semantic retrieval is a viable tool for automatic coding support in official statistics
- centroid-based and disentangled representations are empirically stronger than a single-descriptor baseline
- medium-sized embedding models offer a credible trade-off between performance and operational feasibility

## Main Points To Add

- the study contributes a clearer framing of coding as ranking under NSI constraints
- shortlist generation is the operational interpretation of the results
- future work should strengthen validation, realism of evaluation, and uncertainty-aware decision rules

## Avoid

- generic future-work lists
- overclaiming replacement of existing systems
- restating the results table without synthesis
