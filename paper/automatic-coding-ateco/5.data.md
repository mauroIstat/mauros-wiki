# Data

## Section Goal

Separate observed evidence from methodological design. This section should explain what the datasets are, not yet how they are combined experimentally.

## Suggested Subsections

### 5.1 Real-world dataset: CIRCE-labelled queries

Must cover:

- origin of the queries
- number of observations
- label granularity
- practical value as a realistic benchmark
- caveat: CIRCE labels are a strong operational reference but not a perfect gold standard

### 5.2 Official ATECO material

Must cover:

- hierarchy
- titles and notes
- why the official textual material matters for semantic representation

### 5.3 Synthetic dataset

Must cover:

- generation logic
- quantity
- purpose: lexical enrichment and centroid construction
- limitation: generated queries do not fully reproduce realistic noise, abbreviations, or spelling variation

## Discussion To Anticipate Briefly

- Dataset imbalance across divisions.
- Difference between real user language and official descriptors.
- Difference between real and synthetic queries.

## What To Avoid

- Do not mix data description with model-comparison logic.
- Do not oversell synthetic data as a substitute for real-world coding traffic.
