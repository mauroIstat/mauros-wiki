# Istat’s Governance Framework for AI

Within NSIs, innovation must be supported by a robust governance framework capable of ensuring that new methods and tools remain aligned with the principles of OS. In the case of AI, this need is even more pressing because its adoption occurs within a complex and continuously evolving national and international regulatory landscape. More broadly, the responsible use of AI requires legal, ethical, and methodological dimensions to be addressed jointly rather than separately (High-Level Expert Group on AI, 2019).
For these reasons, the adoption of AI at Istat requires a governance framework that translates general principles into operational rules, processes, and responsibilities. The framework, currently being developed at Istat, is structured around three fundamental and closely interconnected pillars (Fedeli et al., 2025):
- Innovation: Robust procedures are established for overseeing and guiding use case implementation, as well as for comprehensive management of the AI solutions lifecycle. Centralized coordination of projects supports the development of a shared knowledge base and facilitates the reuse of existing solutions across future applications.
- Data & Rules: This pillar addresses the regulatory requirements governing Istat’s use of AI, with a strong emphasis on data quality, availability, and reliability in the development and adoption of AI systems. It also outlines the Institute’s organizational arrangements and best practices for the management and oversight of AI systems.
- People: This pillar focuses on strengthening Istat's internal capabilities through targeted training pathways, capacity-building activities, and structured knowledge sharing.

To support the central governance of AI, Istat has established a multidisciplinary Strategic Committee tasked with translating the three pillars of the framework into concrete operational activities.
Within the Data & Rules pillar, the Committee is working on both general policies addressed to all personnel and more specific guidelines for technical staff operating in areas such as development, procurement, and methodology. This approach is intended to ensure structured oversight and to promote consistency, transparency, and quality in the use and development of AI solutions within Istat.

Activities associated with the Innovation pillar focus on the systematic collection of information, support for developers throughout the lifecycle of AI solutions, and the monitoring of use cases and their performance. One of the first steps in this direction has been the census of AI-related initiatives and use cases currently active within the organization, with the aim of ensuring traceability and proper documentation of applied AI systems. This mapping exercise has identified a broad range of projects, from experimental initiatives and proof-of-concept trials to AI solutions already developed for production. These projects span different phases of the statistical production process and rely on a variety of technologies and methodologies, ranging from machine learning to agent-based AI. In parallel, and in line with the European regulatory framework, Istat is introducing risk-assessment activities inspired by the AI Act, while progressively refining the related evaluation tools.

The People pillar focuses on strengthening Istat's internal capabilities through training and capacity building. Since 2024, Istat has launched a training plan including basic courses on AI technologies and their implications, also in connection with the AI Act. This programme complements specialist training activities introduced in previous years, particularly in machine learning, deep learning, and Python programming. Overall, this pillar aims to support the gradual development of the skills needed to design, assess, and use AI systems within official statistical processes.
