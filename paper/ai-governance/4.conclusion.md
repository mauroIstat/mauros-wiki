In this paper, Istat’s approach to AI-based automatic coding has been illustrated through two use cases: the coding of economic activity descriptions, such as those collected in survey questionnaires, into ATECO categories, and the assignment of PRODCOM codes to enterprise products using company websites as data sources. Taken together, these use cases show that the adoption of AI-based coding in official statistics requires not only robust and controlled methodological innovation, but also a governance framework capable of ensuring reliability, validation, and integration into production processes.

From a methodological perspective, future work will focus on strengthening the validation and reliability of AI-based coding systems. This includes improving benchmark and annotated datasets, refining uncertainty-aware approaches, and developing evaluation procedures that consider not only predictive accuracy, but also consistency, traceability, and suitability for production use. Further work will also be needed to better integrate coding models within end-to-end workflows, especially in cases where coding depends on upstream stages of information extraction and data selection.

From the perspective of AI governance, future initiatives will build on the current implementation of Istat’s governance framework. In particular, these initiatives will be supported by an evaluation questionnaire currently under development to assess the level of awareness of AI use within the Institute and identify the skills required, thereby supporting a structured and targeted strengthening of internal competencies. In parallel, to ensure that the Institute adopts AI in accordance with current regulations and with the principles of accountability, fairness, efficiency, transparency, sustainability, and security, a process has been initiated for certification under ISO/IEC 42001:2023.

More broadly, the experience discussed in this paper suggests that AI-based automatic coding provides a concrete example of how AI methods and tools can be integrated into official statistics. In this sense, the challenge is not simply to adopt more advanced models, but to embed them within statistical processes that remain reliable, transparent, and fully aligned with the principles of official statistics.
