# Conclusion

This paper examined occupation coding in Official Statistics as a semantic retrieval problem over the CP2021 label space. Starting from the institutional difficulty of mapping free-text occupation descriptions to official categories, the study compared multilingual embedding models under progressively richer textual representations of the target classes. The benchmark shows that semantic retrieval is a viable approach for this task and that the strongest overall configuration is obtained with `Qwen/Qwen3-Embedding-4B` and the `5_6_enriched` descriptor setting. More importantly, however, the main contribution of the paper does not lie in model comparison alone. The most robust result is that descriptor enrichment systematically improves retrieval quality, which means that the textual formulation of occupational classes is a central methodological component of retrieval-based coding rather than a secondary implementation detail [AIgov2026] [TrustworthyAI2026].

These findings should be interpreted in line with the scope of the study. The present paper is a benchmark-oriented methodological contribution, not yet a full production evaluation. It establishes that enriching official descriptors with occupational examples and query-derived lexical evidence can substantially improve the semantic alignment between user language and classification language. At the same time, several extensions remain necessary before moving toward a broader operational assessment, including error analysis, subgroup-level evaluation, qualitative inspection of difficult cases, and comparison with stronger baselines or assisted coding workflows. In this sense, the present results provide a compact but solid basis for further research on semantic tools that can support occupation coding while remaining compatible with the methodological requirements of Official Statistics.
