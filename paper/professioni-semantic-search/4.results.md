First, the strongest overall configuration is `Qwen/Qwen3-Embedding-4B` combined with the `tf-idf` descriptors. Second, the most stable improvement does not come from model choice alone, but from progressively enriching the textual representation of the target occupational classes.

To examine the ranking behavior more closely, Figure 1 reports the `Accuracy@k` curves obtained under the `tf-idf` descriptor configuration, that is, the strongest setting in the benchmark. All models improve regularly as \(k\) increases, but their relative ordering is already visible at `Top-1` and remains broadly stable across the full ranking profile. In this setting, `Qwen/Qwen3-Embedding-4B` is the strongest model overall, with `Top-1 = 0.454`, `Top-3 = 0.680`, `Top-5 = 0.753`, `Top-10 = 0.827`, and `MRR = 0.588`. `BAAI/bge-m3` and `Qwen/Qwen3-Embedding-8B` follow closely, but neither surpasses the leading configuration at any of the main cutoffs. This pattern indicates not only that the best-performing models retrieve the correct occupational code more often, but also that they place it more consistently in the top portion of the ranked list.

Figure 2 focuses on the effect of descriptor design. The pattern is clear: moving from `official` to `enriched` improves `Top-1` accuracy for all models, and the subsequent transition to `tf-idf` yields an additional gain in every case. The first enrichment step produces gains ranging from `+2.3` percentage points for `Qwen/Qwen3-Embedding-8B` to `+8.2` points for `BAAI/bge-m3`, while the second step adds between `+1.7` points for `Alibaba-NLP/gte-multilingual-base` and `+9.1` points for `Qwen/Qwen3-Embedding-0.6B`. The main result is therefore not tied to a single architecture. Rather, it emerges from a more informative verbalization of the target occupational classes, which improves the semantic alignment between real occupation descriptions and the CP2021 descriptor space.

From a methodological perspective, this is the central result of the benchmark. Model choice matters, but descriptor design is the more robust driver of retrieval performance across systems. This is especially relevant for occupation coding, where operational usefulness often depends less on a single forced prediction than on the ability to provide a short and plausible list of candidate codes. In this respect, the enriched descriptor settings, and especially the `tf-idf` condition, improve precisely the aspect of retrieval quality that matters most for interactive coding support.
