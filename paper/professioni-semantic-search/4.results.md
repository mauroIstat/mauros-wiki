# Results

This section focuses on the two figures that are most informative for a short-paper format: the `Accuracy@k` curves for the strongest descriptor configuration and the model-by-model comparison of progressive descriptor enrichment. Together, they summarize both overall ranking quality and the specific contribution of descriptor design.

## Overall performance in the best descriptor setting

Figure 1 reports the `Accuracy@k` curves for the `5_6_enriched` descriptor set, that is, the most informative representation tested in this benchmark. This condition combines the official 5-digit CP2021 description, the aggregated 6-digit occupational examples, and the TF-IDF terms extracted from the labelled query corpus.

The main pattern is clear. All models improve steadily as `k` increases, but the ranking of systems is already visible at very small cutoffs. `Qwen/Qwen3-Embedding-4B` is the best model throughout the curve and reaches `Top-1 = 0.454`, `Top-3 = 0.680`, `Top-5 = 0.753`, and `Top-10 = 0.827`, with `MRR = 0.588`. The next strongest systems are `BAAI/bge-m3`, `Qwen/Qwen3-Embedding-8B`, and `intfloat/multilingual-e5-base`, which remain close to the leading model but do not surpass it at any of the reported cutoffs.

Two aspects of Figure 1 are especially relevant for interpretation. First, the differences among models emerge already at `k = 1`, which means that the strongest systems do not simply recover the correct code somewhere in the candidate list, but place it at the top more often. Second, the curves remain well separated through `k = 10` and `k = 20`, suggesting that the best-performing models are consistently better at ranking the correct occupational class near the top, rather than benefiting only from larger candidate lists.

## Effect of progressive descriptor enrichment

Figure 2 summarizes one of the strongest findings of the benchmark: retrieval quality improves systematically when the class descriptors are enriched. The figure compares `Top-1` accuracy across the three descriptor conditions:

- `5digit`: official 5-digit descriptors only
- `5_6digit`: 5-digit descriptors plus aggregated 6-digit occupational examples
- `5_6_enriched`: `5_6digit` plus query-derived TF-IDF terms

The result is consistent across all evaluated models. Every model improves when moving from `5digit` to `5_6digit`, and every model improves again when moving from `5_6digit` to `5_6_enriched`. This pattern is important because it shows that the gains are not limited to a single architecture, but reflect a robust effect of descriptor design.

The size of the gains is also substantively meaningful. From `5digit` to `5_6digit`, the increase in `Top-1` accuracy ranges from `+2.3` percentage points for `Qwen/Qwen3-Embedding-8B` to `+8.2` points for `BAAI/bge-m3`. The additional transition from `5_6digit` to `5_6_enriched` yields further gains ranging from `+1.7` points for `Alibaba-NLP/gte-multilingual-base` to `+9.1` points for `Qwen/Qwen3-Embedding-0.6B`. The best overall configuration is again `Qwen/Qwen3-Embedding-4B` with `Top-1 = 0.454` in the fully enriched setting.

For a short paper, Figure 2 supports a compact but strong claim: the largest and most stable improvement in occupational coding performance comes from enriching the textual representation of the target classes. Model choice still matters, but descriptor enrichment produces a broad upward shift across the full benchmark.

## Main findings

Three findings are especially robust and suitable for a concise conference paper.

1. The best overall system is `Qwen/Qwen3-Embedding-4B` evaluated on the `5_6_enriched` descriptors.
2. Descriptor enrichment improves performance consistently across all models, regardless of baseline strength.
3. The benefit of enrichment is visible not only in `Top-1`, but across the full ranking profile, including `Top-3`, `Top-5`, `Top-10`, and `MRR`.

Taken together, the results indicate that the textual formulation of CP2021 class descriptors is a central factor in retrieval-based occupation coding. In this benchmark, improving descriptor quality is at least as important as changing the underlying embedding model and, in practice, is the most stable source of performance gain across systems.
