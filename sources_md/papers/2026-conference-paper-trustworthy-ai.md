Towards Trustworthy AI in Official Statistics:
Methodological Challenges and Research
Perspectives from Istat
Mauro Bruno1
Italian National Institute of Statistics (Istat), via Cesare Balbo 16, 00184 Rome
Abstract.Artificial Intelligence (AI) is increasingly transforming the
production and dissemination of Official Statistics. While AI technolo-
gies offer significant opportunities to improve efficiency, exploit new data
sources, and enhance access to statistical information, their adoption
raises critical methodological challenges related to validation, transparency,
and privacy protection. This paper examines the role of trustworthy AI
in Official Statistics, focusing on the methodological implications of in-
tegrating AI-based methods into statistical production processes. After
outlining AI opportunities across the phases of the statistical produc-
tion process, the paper identifies key challenges related to validating
AI-based methods, adapting quality frameworks, and the responsible
use of data. Building on ongoing research at the Italian National In-
stitute of Statistics (Istat), the paper proposes an integrated approach
that combines methodological research, institutional governance, capac-
ity building, and collaboration with public research infrastructures. This
approach provides a practical framework for supporting the responsi-
ble and sustainable adoption of AI in Official Statistics while preserving
statistical quality and public trust.
Keywords:Artificial Intelligence, Trustworthy AI, Official Statistics
1 Introduction
Over the past decade, the environment in which National Statistical Institutes
(NSIs) operate has undergone a profound transformation. The digitalization of
society and the rapid progress of Artificial Intelligence (AI) are reshaping how
data is created, accessed, and used. Data ecosystems have become increasingly
complex, and expectations for Official Statistics have grown [1]. Users now de-
mand more timely releases, greater levels of detail, and simpler ways to access
and interact with statistical information.
However, the adoption of AI in Official Statistics also raises important method-
ological and institutional challenges. NSIs operate within established quality
frameworks and legal constraints designed to ensure reliability, transparency,
and the protection of privacy and confidentiality, as reflected in the European
Statistics Code of Practice [2].

AI-based approaches, particularly when relying on complex or black-box
models, may introduce new risks related to validation, interpretability, data
protection, and potential biases in data or algorithms. Addressing these risks
while ensuring alignment with the core principles of Official Statistics—such as
methodological soundness, transparency, and public trust—represents a central
challenge for statistical organizations [3].
This paper examines the opportunities and methodological challenges of
adopting trustworthy AI in Official Statistics, drawing on ongoing research at
Istat. Specifically, it focuses on key issues such as the validation of AI-based
methods, the adaptation of quality frameworks to AI-driven processes, and the
responsible use of data in statistical production. It also presents examples of cur-
rent research at Istat aimed at integrating AI techniques into statistical processes
while preserving statistical quality and reliability.
2 AI Opportunities in Official Statistics
Artificial Intelligence is increasingly being utilized by National Statistical Insti-
tutes to support various activities throughout the statistical production process.

cessing, and data dissemination.

Data collection.AI technologies can enhance data collection in multiple ways.
Intelligent systems can assist respondents in completing questionnaires, support
interviewers through smart tools, and facilitate the integration of administrative
and non-traditional data sources into production processes. These approaches

can reduce response burden and data collection costs, improve data quality, and
broaden the range of information available for statistical processing.
Data processing.AI techniques enable the transformation of complex and het-

matic text classification, detection of non-sampling errors, and imputation of
missing values in large and complex datasets. These methods allow statistical
offices to process large volumes of data more efficiently while maintaining estab-

Data dissemination.AI provides new opportunities to improve access to Offi-
cial Statistics. Natural language interfaces and AI assistants can help users more
easily retrieve relevant statistical information, generate personalized tables and
visualizations, and interact with statistical content more intuitively. However,
these advancements must still adhere to the principles of privacy and confiden-

Overall, the adoption of AI across these phases reflects a broader shift towards
more flexible, scalable, and data-driven statistical production systems that can
leverage both traditional and non-traditional data sources.
3 Methodological Challenges for Trustworthy AI
While AI offers significant opportunities across the statistical production pro-
cess, its adoption in Official Statistics also introduces important methodological
challenges. Recent work within the international statistical community has high-
lighted several critical issues related to the validation of AI-based methods, the
adaptation of quality frameworks, and the responsible use of data in AI-driven
statistical systems [1, 4, 5].
V alidation and statistical reliability .A key challenge is validating AI-based
methods used in statistical production. Many AI models rely on complex learn-
ing mechanisms that can be opaque, with outputs that may vary depending on
the training data, model parameters, or prompting strategies. To ensure robust-
ness, reproducibility, and stability of results, it is essential to develop validation
procedures that adhere to established statistical quality standards.
Quality frameworks for AI-driven statistics.Existing quality frameworks
for Official Statistics were largely developed for structured data sources and
traditional production pipelines. The increasing use of AI methods and non-
traditional data sources requires adapting these frameworks to account for new
sources of uncertainty and error. These include the probabilistic nature of AI
models, dependence on training data, and potential biases in both data and al-
gorithms. In this context, quality assessment must evolve to incorporate new di-
mensions, such as evaluating model performance under different data conditions,
assessing the robustness of results to changes in training data or model configu-
rations, and detecting and mitigating systematic biases. This also requires defin-
ing appropriate quality indicators, monitoring mechanisms, and methodological
guidelines tailored to AI-assisted statistical production.

Privacy and responsible data use.The use of AI in Official Statistics raises
critical issues related not only to privacy protection but also to the operational
context in which AI systems are deployed. In particular, the increasing reliance
on externally developed models, cloud-based infrastructure, and API-based ser-
vices introduces new risks regarding data confidentiality, data control, and com-
pliance with legal and institutional constraints. The use of sensitive data within
AI systems requires careful consideration of where and how data is processed.
While AI models deployed in controlled, on-premises environments may be
compatible with existing confidentiality requirements, using external APIs or
shared cloud services may raise significant concerns, depending on data resi-
dency, access policies, and system architecture. Addressing these challenges re-
quires robust data governance frameworks, clear rules governing the allowed use
of AI technologies, and technical safeguards to ensure that sensitive data re-
mains protected throughout the processing pipeline. These aspects are essential
to ensure that AI-supported processes remain aligned with the fundamental prin-
ciples of Official Statistics, particularly confidentiality, security, and institutional
accountability
4 An Institutional Ecosystem for Trustworthy AI at Istat
Building on the challenges outlined above, Istat is addressing AI adoption through
a combination of applied research, experimental projects, and strategic collabo-
rations with other research bodies. These activities aim to explore how AI tech-
niques can be integrated into statistical production while preserving statistical
quality, transparency, and reliability, as discussed in recent methodological con-
tributions developed within the Official Statistics community [6]. Initiatives are
largely coordinated within the Directorate for Methodology and focus on testing
AI methods in real statistical contexts, assessing their methodological implica-
tions, and identifying the conditions under which they can be safely integrated
into production processes.
A primary area of work concerns AI-supported statistical production. Ongo-
ing projects investigate the use of Machine Learning (ML) techniques for auto-
matic classification tasks, such as coding economic activities, industrial products,
and causes of death. Recent work on product classification for PRODCOM sur-
veys highlights the potential of AI-driven approaches for transforming web-based
or textual data into structured statistical variables [7].
A second area of research investigates the use of new and non-traditional
data sources for statistical purposes. Current experiments include analyzing en-
terprise websites, Earth observation data, Mobile Network Operators data, and
patent documents, with the aim of broadening the informational basis of Offi-
cial Statistics and enabling new forms of statistical measurement [8, 9]. These
data sources pose significant methodological challenges, such as issues with data
quality, coverage, and interpretability. AI techniques can assist in extracting
structured information from these complex sources, but their application re-

quires careful methodological evaluation to ensure that the resulting indicators
meet official statistics standards [4].
Research is also being conducted on synthetic data generation, exploring
how AI-based techniques can complement traditional statistical disclosure con-
trol methods to reduce disclosure risk while preserving analytical utility. Beyond
research activities, Istat is establishing institutional mechanisms to promote re-
sponsible use of AI. These include internal guidelines and methodological rec-
ommendations to ensure that AI-based applications adhere to the principles of
Official Statistics. Governance plays a key role in coordinating experimental ac-
tivities, supporting methodological validation, and ensuring that innovation is
implemented in a controlled and sustainable way.
A further component concerns collaboration with public research infrastruc-
tures. At the national level, partnerships with organizations such as CINECA
provide access to high-performance computing environments for large-scale ex-
perimentation with advanced AI models. At the international level, Istat collab-
orates with other NSIs within initiatives funded by Eurostat and the European
Statistical System, focusing on topics such as AI/ML for Official Statistics [10],
the integration of mobile phone data with traditional surveys [11], and the use
of online job advertisements for labour market analysis [12].
Finally, the adoption of AI in Official Statistics requires strengthening inter-
nal competencies. Istat is therefore investing in capacity-building initiatives in
areas such as machine learning, data science, and AI governance. Training and
knowledge-sharing activities support the diffusion of these skills across the In-
stitute, ensuring that AI systems can be effectively understood, evaluated, and
used by statistical experts.
5 Conclusions
Artificial Intelligence offers significant opportunities to improve the use of non-
traditional data sources and to enhance the production and dissemination of
Official Statistics. However, its adoption must remain aligned with the funda-
mental principles of statistical systems, especially methodological soundness,
transparency, and confidentiality protection.
The main challenge for National Statistical Institutes is not just adopting AI
technologies but ensuring that their integration into statistical processes remains
trustworthy and methodologically sound. Istat’s experience shows how this goal
can be achieved through an integrated approach that combines methodologi-
cal research, experimental activities, institutional governance, and collaboration
with public research infrastructures. Moving forward, more work is needed to de-
velop validation protocols for AI-based methods, adapt statistical quality frame-
works to AI-driven processes, and create approaches to evaluate robustness, bias,
and uncertainty. Ongoing collaboration within the European Statistical System
and the wider international community will be vital to support the responsible
and sustainable use of AI in Official Statistics.
