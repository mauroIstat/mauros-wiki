This note frames AI in official statistics mainly as a modernization and dissemination challenge that must remain aligned with trust, transparency, privacy, and institutional quality requirements.

## Source details

- Source filename: `raw/notes/2025-note-official-statistics-ai.pdf`
- Document type: conference note / workshop paper
- Probable context/year: UNECE HLG-MOS Workshop on the Modernisation of Official Statistics, 2025
- Tone/use context: institutional with methodological framing

## Extracted content

- The note argues that users expect official statistics to be timely, accessible, transparent, and easy to query.
- IstatData is presented as a central dissemination platform.
- Two AI-oriented functionalities are highlighted: natural-language dataset search and real-time analysis/visualization support.
- The text repeatedly emphasizes privacy, transparency, regulation, and the need to adapt quality standards.
- The modernization agenda is framed as ongoing and dependent on research capacity, skills, and tooling.

## Key messages

- AI is discussed as a means to improve access to official statistics, not only internal production.
- The institutional challenge is dual: innovate on usability while preserving trust.
- Machine-readable dissemination and user-friendly access are treated as legitimate goals inside the quality mission of official statistics.

## Reusable formulations

- Official statistics must remain accessible and trusted by all users.
- The challenge is not simply to add AI tools, but to embed them in transparent and quality-aware dissemination processes.
- Research, skills, and modern tools are presented as enabling conditions for relevance and reliability.
