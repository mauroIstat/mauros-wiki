## Extracted content

- The chapter presents Istat's trajectory from early experimentation toward production pipelines aligned with Trusted Smart Statistics principles.
- Big-data sources include satellite imagery, web data, maritime traffic data, and sensor-derived information.
- The text discusses projects on maritime traffic using AIS data, urban green-area estimation with remote sensing, and web intelligence for business statistics.
- Methodological issues are discussed around estimation, imputation, and quality assessment, not only around classification.
- The chapter also emphasizes changing skill requirements, investments in human capital, and participation in international research projects.

## Key messages

- The corpus contains a broader institutional innovation layer than the first semantic-retrieval-centered pass suggested.
- Machine learning at Istat is tied to production modernization, not only to NLP and coding tasks.
- Trusted Smart Statistics acts as a bridge between new data sources and official-statistics principles.

## Reusable formulations

- Innovation is described as a shift from experimentation toward new production pipelines.
- Big Data and machine learning are valuable when they improve timeliness, granularity, and quality without leaving governance frameworks behind.
