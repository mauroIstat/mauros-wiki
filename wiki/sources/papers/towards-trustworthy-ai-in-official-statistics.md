## Extracted content

- The paper surveys AI opportunities across the statistical production and dissemination process.
- It foregrounds methodological challenges around validation, transparency, and privacy.
- Existing quality frameworks are treated as constraints that AI systems must satisfy rather than bypass.
- The proposed response is integrated: methodological research, governance, skills, and collaboration with research infrastructures.
- The text repeatedly links trustworthy AI to preservation of public trust in official statistics.

## Key messages

- AI is framed as institutionally consequential, not merely technically promising.
- Adoption requires adaptation of quality frameworks rather than ad hoc experimentation alone.
- Responsible use of data is treated as part of trustworthy AI, not a separate compliance afterthought.

## Reusable formulations

- Responsible and sustainable AI adoption must preserve statistical quality and public trust.
- Methodological research and institutional governance are complementary, not competing, requirements.
- Trustworthy AI in official statistics is a practical operating framework, not only a normative label.
