## Extracted content

- The paper frames statistical classifications such as NACE as central infrastructure for NSOs.
- Deterministic coding systems are described as accurate but labor-intensive and hard to generalize across domains.
- Language models and semantic-search systems are proposed as more scalable alternatives.
- Uncertainty quantification is identified as the main methodological bottleneck.
- The paper addresses this through conformal prediction, including discussion of coverage by ATECO division.

## Key messages

- This source strengthens the chronology of the wiki: conformal-prediction concerns appear clearly before the later chapter.
- Language models are not presented as a free-standing generative tool but as part of an automated coding and semantic-search pipeline.
- Scalability gains are balanced against reliability requirements.

## Reusable formulations

- Deterministic systems generalize poorly because each classification requires its own rule set.
- Language models create opportunities for efficiency and scalability, but uncertainty quantification remains essential.
- Automated coding in official statistics is constrained by statistical validity, not only by predictive accuracy.
