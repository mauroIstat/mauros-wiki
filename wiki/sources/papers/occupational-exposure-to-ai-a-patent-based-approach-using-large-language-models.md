## Extracted content

- The paper follows a patent-based approach to quantify occupational exposure to AI using ISCO-08 data.
- Patent text is semantically mapped to occupations through large-language-model-assisted similarity analysis.
- The highest exposure is reported for ICT-related professions, with later diffusion toward managerial, creative, and process-automation roles.
- Temporal analysis is used to describe expansion from technical sectors in the 2000s to broader business and creative domains in the 2010s and 2020s.
- The conclusions emphasize workforce policy implications, reskilling, and AI governance.

## Key messages

- The patent line in the corpus is not only about sector mapping; it also supports labor-market indicators.
- The paper extends the corpus from methodological mapping to substantive exposure analysis.
- Governance enters here through labor-market stability and reskilling needs rather than only official-statistics validation.

## Reusable formulations

- AI exposure expands over time from technical occupations toward broader organizational and creative domains.
- Patent-based semantic analysis can support indicator construction on emerging technological exposure.
- Policy relevance is linked to reskilling and governance, not just measurement novelty.
