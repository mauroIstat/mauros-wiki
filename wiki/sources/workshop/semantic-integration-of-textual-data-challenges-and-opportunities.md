## Extracted content

- The deck opens with AI-based integration of questionnaire text, enterprise websites, and patent data.
- Web scraping is presented as a long-running Istat line of work starting in 2013.
- The statistical objective is to derive indicators on firms' digitalization and related characteristics.
- Website identification is singled out as a critical early-stage modeling problem.
- Enterprise descriptions are linked to statistical categories through embedding-based semantic comparison.
- Reliability assessment again appears via confidence thresholds, prediction sets, and uncertainty quantification.

## Key messages

- The corpus does not treat web scraping as a standalone engineering task; it is a statistical integration pipeline.
- Website acquisition quality shapes downstream inference quality.
- The deck aligns web data work with the same reliability vocabulary used for coding and patent mapping.

## Reusable formulations

- Web data is rich, but difficult to systematically collect and standardize.
- The challenge is not only to use AI, but to use it responsibly in official statistics.
- New opportunities for data integration must still satisfy validation and quality requirements.
