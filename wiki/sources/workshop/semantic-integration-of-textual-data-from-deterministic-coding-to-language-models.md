## Extracted content

- Official statistics are defined as relying on structured classifications such as NACE and ISCO.
- Administrative records, web data, and patents are presented as text-rich but poorly aligned with those classifications.
- Traditional coding systems are described as dictionary- and rule-based.
- Embeddings are introduced as numerical representations enabling semantic rather than lexical matching.
- Reliability is treated as a separate question addressed through confidence thresholds, prediction sets, and uncertainty quantification.
- The deck closes by stressing new institutional skills requirements.

## Key messages

- The corpus links semantic integration with institutional skill change.
- Reliability is framed as a methodological obligation, not a cosmetic add-on.
- Language-model-era methods are introduced through continuity with statistical classifications, not as a break from them.

## Reusable formulations

- Official statistics transform real-world phenomena into structured categories.
- These systems are essential for consistency, comparability, and integration.
- AI predictions must be supported by quality assessment, uncertainty quantification, and robust statistical validation procedures.
