Trustworthy AI in this corpus is grounded in validation, uncertainty handling, transparency, privacy protection, and institutional responsibility.

## Strongly grounded elements

- AI outputs must be validated rather than accepted as inherently reliable.
- Statistical institutions must preserve quality frameworks and public trust.
- Privacy and responsible data use are part of the AI discussion, especially in institutional and governance-oriented sources.
- Reliability mechanisms include confidence thresholds, prediction sets, semantic confidence, and conformal prediction.

## Important distinction

The corpus uses "trustworthy" in a practical institutional sense. It is less about abstract AI ethics language and more about whether methods can be defended inside official-statistics workflows.
