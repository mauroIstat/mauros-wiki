Across the corpus, AI in official statistics is presented as both an opportunity for modernization and a source of methodological obligations.

## Strongly grounded themes

- AI is used to improve access to statistical information, not only internal production workflows.
- Text-rich and non-traditional data sources are a repeated focus: questionnaire text, enterprise websites, patents, and administrative text.
- The corpus treats classifications as the stable backbone that AI systems must work with rather than replace.
- Innovation is repeatedly paired with public trust, transparency, privacy, and quality frameworks.
- The expanded corpus now shows that AI and ML at Istat also cover remote sensing, AIS data, estimation, imputation, and broader big-data production pipelines.

## Cross-document synthesis

- The note on IstatData stresses dissemination and user accessibility.
- The trustworthy AI paper adds a governance and validation frame.
- The chapter and workshop decks provide concrete operational use cases such as ATECO coding, semantic search, web intelligence, and PRODCOM-related web classification.
- The 2025 big-data chapter broadens the institutional picture beyond NLP, while the 2025 language-model paper gives an earlier methodological account of uncertainty-aware coding.

## Tensions preserved in the corpus

- innovation vs methodological safeguards
- broader accessibility vs statistical defensibility
- automation potential vs need for human validation
