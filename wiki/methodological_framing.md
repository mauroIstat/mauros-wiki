# Methodological Framing

## Default framing for introductions

A common introduction pattern is:

1. Describe a structural change in the environment of Official Statistics: digitization, new data ecosystems, changing user expectations, or rapid AI progress.
2. State the institutional tension: NSIs must innovate while preserving trust, quality, privacy, and transparency.
3. Identify a concrete bottleneck involving text, classification, or access to information.
4. Present the proposed method as a practical way to modernize statistical production or dissemination under quality constraints.

This framing appears in the trustworthy-AI paper, the AI-governance automatic-coding paper, the semantic-search chapter, the AI-in-Official-Statistics note, and the automated-coding paper.

## Default framing for methods

Methods are usually presented as modular workflows rather than as abstract algorithms. The preferred style is to explain:

- the statistical task
- the data source
- the classification or target structure
- the representation method
- the retrieval or prediction mechanism
- the validation or uncertainty layer

This modular style is especially clear in the semantic-search, PRODCOM, cause-of-death, and anti-violence-network material. The newer papers add two useful variants: model comparison inside an existing production workflow, as in Iris-based ICD-10 coding, and conservative LLM extraction where each structured output must be tied to explicit documentary evidence.

## Preferred contribution style

The corpus usually avoids exaggerated novelty claims. Contributions are positioned as methodological, operational, or institutional advances such as:

- showing that a semantic-search approach is feasible in an Official Statistics setting
- improving scalability relative to deterministic systems
- providing defensible uncertainty estimates
- integrating non-traditional data into production workflows
- supporting dissemination and user access without abandoning quality requirements

## Preferred limitation style

Limitations are typically framed in terms of validation, robustness, interpretability, data quality, privacy, infrastructure, and institutional sustainability. Even when results are strong, the writing tends to qualify claims by stressing that quality assurance, expert review, and governance remain necessary.

## Reusable argumentative moves

- Move from broad institutional change to a narrow, operational bottleneck.
- Contrast traditional deterministic methods with more flexible semantic approaches without dismissing the former.
- Treat uncertainty estimation as a requirement for adoption, not a technical afterthought.
- Emphasize that AI extends the informational basis of Official Statistics while also creating new quality risks.
- Present LLM-based extraction as a complement to survey data when documents contain actors, roles, or attributes that respondents may omit or aggregate.
- When working with official coding pipelines, identify which phase AI supports rather than describing the whole workflow as generically automated.
- Close by returning to responsible adoption, methodological control, and public trust.
