This snippet captures a recurring teaching move: embeddings are introduced by contrasting word matching with meaning matching.

## Reusable block

Traditional systems often compare text using exact words or predefined dictionaries. Embedding-based methods work differently: they convert texts into numerical representations where semantically similar expressions are placed close together. This makes it possible to retrieve related meanings even when the wording changes, although additional validation is still needed before using those outputs in high-stakes settings.

## Use cases

- introductory teaching slides
- workshop explanations for non-specialist audiences
- short background sections in talks
