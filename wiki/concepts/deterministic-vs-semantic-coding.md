One of the strongest distinctions in the corpus contrasts deterministic coding based on lexical overlap with semantic coding based on learned representations.

## Extracted distinction

- Deterministic coding uses dictionaries, parsing rules, and word-overlap matching.
- Semantic coding uses embeddings and similarity in a shared representation space.
- Deterministic systems preserve explicitness but miss semantic variation.
- Semantic systems capture meaning better but require validation, domain adaptation, and reliability controls.

## Why it matters in this corpus

- It structures both scientific argument and teaching explanation.
- It is used to explain why official-statistics pipelines need updated methods for modern text-rich data sources.
